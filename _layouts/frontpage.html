The Bible: It's all about Jesus...

Most religious institutions are about things people do, or should do, or even more overwhelmingly about things they shouldn't do. The focus is on people. That's why God's business is big business. Many products are created to supposedly bring the believers closer to God. From books, to music, seminars, conferences, training, you name it. The abundance of these things, and the pursuit after them, say one thing: Jesus saved you, but now it's your turn to take it all the way. You've heard about Jesus at an evangelistic meeting, now we, the churches of Jesus Christ, will tell you about yourselves. The focus is now on you.

But the true church of Christ is never meant to be like this. It sets people free to live the lives God planned for them. Its sole purpose is to help folks fix their eyes on Jesus, not on themselves, to fully realize that Jesus died on the cross for them once and never again, that they were made whole to face God without shame, with heads lifted high, and hear the gentle whisper of the Spirit of God that "now there is no condemnation to those who are in Christ Jesus." (Romans 8:1)
